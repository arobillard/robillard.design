Cujo

Not just a lap dog.

Cujo comes from Stephen King’s psychological horror novel and movie of the same name. He is a dog that is infected by rabies and attacks and kills a number of people in a small community. The name "Cujo" is also known for being the nickname of a famous NHL hockey goalie Curtis “Cujo” Joseph.

The symbol design I have created represent both of these famous Cujos in different ways. It represents the Cujo novel by having the shape and features of a rabid dog barring its teeth, leering, and having blood dripping from its mouth. It represents Curtis Joseph by wearing an old fashioned goalie mask. An old fashioned goalie mask was used to represent Curtis Joseph’s tie to hockey over a modern mask because it also alludes to classic horror genre imagery. In the film Cujo frequently forces his snout through car windows and other areas while attacking people. The mask is broken and cracking to show that the dogs head is trying to force its way through.
